Lost Cities

We are about to seek treasure in a lost city, but every expedition comes at a steep price. You can also enlist local companions, which doubles both the risk and the reward in the end.

This version is adapted from the original by Reiner Knizia and can be played with a standard deck of playing cards.

Game Setup

  1. Take a standard deck of playing cards and remove 4 Aces, placing them in the center of the table to represent the four lost cities.
  2. Deal 1 Joker to each player. Shuffle the remaining cards to form a draw pile.
  3. Each player starts by drawing 5 cards, so together with the Joker, everyone has 6 cards in hand at the start.

Game Play

Players take turns. On your turn, play one card then draw one card.

When playing a card, it must match the suit of one of the Aces:

  • Play it on your side of the Ace to signify exploring that city, or
  • Place it on top of the Ace (i.e., on the city’s discard pile) to discard that card.

When drawing a card, you may:

  • Draw the top card from the draw pile, or
  • Take the top card from any city’s discard pile.

As soon as the draw pile is empty, the round ends.

Exploring with Cards

Each player’s exploration is independent; you only consider the cards on your side for scoring.

  • 2–10 (Number Cards): Except for the first card you play in that city, each subsequent number card must be strictly higher than the previous number card used in that city.
  • Joker (Wild Card):
    • May substitute for any suit’s number card.
    • Can only be played immediately after a 2–9.
    • Its “value” is considered the previous card’s value +1.
  • J, Q, K (Multiplier Cards):
    • Must be played before any number cards are placed for that city.
    • Playing 1/2/3 of these multiplier cards makes the final score for that city multiply by 2/3/4 respectively.

Scoring

For each city you explored, calculate:
(Sum of Number Cards – Cost) × Multiplier
The cost is 20. Sum all city scores you have (for each city you explored) to get your total exploration score.

It is recommended to play three rounds. The player with the highest total score across all rounds wins.
